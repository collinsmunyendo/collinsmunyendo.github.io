About

I am a 2nd year Master student at Department of Informatics, University of California, Irvine. I currently work with Dr. Anne Marie Piper. I received my B.Phil. at Fudan University in 2021.

Research Interests

My research aims to make technology more inclusive to people who are marginalized in digital environments, e.g., older adults, d/Deaf people. I have been mainly working on the following two questions:

  • How to improve digital and information equity among information- and/or digitally constrained communities?
    • I have focused on collaboration within people's existing support networks (e.g., family, pre-established communities) to facilitate people's information and/or digital access. So far, I have looked into family support to older adults' technology learning and use, and d/Deaf communities' support to the growth of online sign language content.

  • How to improve equity and inclusiveness in online participation?
    • I often observe online social spaces to understand people from their self- disclosure and expression. I see the potential of online social platforms and communities for people to express their needs and build their communities to promote their messages. However, I found that many populations often seem to be invisible online (e.g., older adults), even though Internet seems to be an open place for all.

    Therefore, I have been always thinking about how to improve equity and inclusiveness in online participation. So far, I have focused on older adults active in online participation, to see how the platforms they use support their participation. Specifically, I have examined two cases, i.e., older adult content creators on short video platforms, and an online community where inter-generational communications are active.
