I received my B.Phil. at Fudan University in 2021.

Research Interests

My research aims to make technology more inclusive to people who are marginalized in digital environments, e.g., older adults, d/Deaf people. I have been mainly working on the following two questions:

  • How to improve digital and/or information equity among information- and/or digitally constrained communities?
    • I focused on collaboration within people's existing support networks (e.g., family, pre-established communities). So far, I have looked into family support to older adults' technology learning and use [CSCW2022 (1st author)], and d/Deaf communities' support to the growth of online sign language content.

  • How to improve equity and inclusiveness in online participation?
    • As a way of knowing about people, I often observe online social spaces. Therefore, I have also been thinking about equity in online participation. So far, I have focused on older adults active in online participation, to see how the platforms they use support their participation. I have examined two cases, i.e., older adult content creators on short video platforms, and an online community where inter-generational communications are active.
