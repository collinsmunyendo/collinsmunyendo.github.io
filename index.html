About Me

I am a final year Computer Science PhD Candidate in the Usable Security and Privacy Lab at the George Washington University where I am fortunate to be advised by Adam Aviv. My research interests are broadly in the area of human-centered security and privacy. My work combines interdisciplinary qualitative and quantitative methods to understand and improve the security, privacy, and safety of a wide range of users in three main ways:

  1. Authentication: I investigate how users authenticate across multiple domains, designing systems that improve both security and usability.
  2. Overlooked Users: I explore the unique needs of overlooked groups e.g., majority world users, developing solutions that protect all users.
  3. Tech Harms: I study harms caused by current and emerging technologies e.g., AI and predatory apps, finding ways to protect users.
I am on the job market seeking research roles in academia or industry!
